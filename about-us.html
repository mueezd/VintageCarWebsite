History

In the world of automobiles, the vintage era was a period of transition. In 1919, the car was still considered a rarity, but by 1930, it was well on its way to becoming commonplace. Indeed, the level of automotive production at the conclusion of this century was not surpassed until the 1950s. Most industrialized countries established nationwide road systems in the ensuing years so that by the conclusion of the period, the capacity to negotiate unpaved roads was no longer a major factor in vehicle design.

During this time, automobiles became far more practical, convenient, and comfortable. The in-car radio was also introduced, as was car heating (which progressed from hot bricks via double-skinned exhaust manifolds to the heater radiator matrix). Braking with all four wheels.

Who We Are

Vintage and historic cars elicit enthusiasm and ignite a love, and the Vintage Car Club has provided a competitive and social setting for people to enjoy pre-war automobiles since 1934. The Club is one of the world's largest and most well-respected motoring groups, active in every element of pre-war car motoring and motorsport.There are many of opportunities to become involved, whether as a car owner, marshal, competitor, or enthusiast, with such a varied range of motoring disciplines on offer.
